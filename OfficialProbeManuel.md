A **probe** is a small, controlled action used to **measure reality**.

**CRITICAL PRE-REQUISITE: BIOLOGICAL SIGNAL INTEGRITY**
Before any probe, ensure your own computational power is optimum.

- **Memory (Sleep)**
- **Energy (Eat)**

Loop: **Model → Probe → Measure → Update → Repeat**

## 1) Core terminology (universal)

- **System**: anything with inputs/outputs and feedback (market, audience, body, team, algorithm).
- **Mission**: long-term objective (why).
- **Operation**: a campaign composed of multiple probes (how).

## 2) The 3 Layers of Probing (The Funnel)

To convert interest into outcomes, move from wide to narrow:

### Layer 1: Global Probes (Market-wide)

- **Action:** Broad tests or public rankings/indices to establish authority.
- **Goal:** Filter the crowd and identify who is "active" or "triggered" by the signal.
- **Example:** A public list of the "Top 50 Luton Rappers" to see who engages.

### Layer 2: Segment Probes (Tribe/Cluster)

- **Action:** Focused tests on specific groups or "nodes" identified in Layer 1.
- **Goal:** Map the internal network and find the gatekeepers/decision-makers.
- **Example:** Analyzing the followers/collabs of the top 5 rappers from Layer 1.

### Layer 3: Personal Probes (Single Target)

- **Action:** Direct, one-on-one measurement (DMs, voice notes, calls).
- **Goal:** Identify a specific latent need and offer a micro-commitment fix.
- **Example:** Sending a specific mix note to one rapper to prove competence.

### Layer 4: The Shadow Layer (Gatekeeper Bypass)

- **Definition:** The friction point between a Segment (The Company) and the Personal (The Person).
- **Signal:** You are screaming into a void (ignored DMs, automated rejections).
- **The Move:** Stop probing the entity; probe for the **Human Proxy**. Identify the name, the personality, or the "backdoor" entry point.

### Layer 5: The Meta-Layer (Internal Audit)

- **Definition:** Probing the prober. Measuring the effectiveness of your own machine.
- **Signal:** Repeated high Delta (Δ) in debriefs. The "Moves" are happening but the results aren't changing.
- **The Move:** Pivot the **Manual**, not the Target. Audit your own Biological, Environmental, and Psychological systems.

## 3) Hypothesis stage (mandatory)

A hypothesis ties variables into a testable claim:

> If **X** is applied to **Y** under **Z**, then **O** changes because **R**.

- **X (Intervention/Input)**: what you do
- **Y (Target/Population)**: who/what you apply it to
- **Z (Context/Conditions)**: when/where/constraints
- **O (Output/KPI)**: what you measure
- **R (Mechanism)**: why it should work

**Rule:** change **one variable at a time** or you learn nothing.

## 3) Systems-based variables (universal set)

Use these as hypotheses only. Trust them only when validated via proxies.

### A) Biological system

- Latent: fatigue, arousal, sleep debt
- Proxies: time-of-day performance, consistency, late-night spikes

### B) Psychological system

- Latent: status threat, belonging need, autonomy drive, insecurity
- Proxies: language (“need”, “anyone?”), defensiveness, validation-seeking

### C) Social system

- Latent: gatekeeper pressure, social proof hunger
- Proxies: repost graph, collabs, who they respond to

### D) Economic system

- Latent: spending power, price sensitivity
- Proxies: conversion by price, compliance with “send budget”, timing cycles

### E) Information system

- Latent: ranking forces, distribution bias
- Proxies: retention, saves/shares, reach volatility

### F) Environmental system (The Laboratory)

- Latent: Noise floor, focus-drain, standard-drift, seasonality.
- Proxies: Desk state, room temperature, notification density, "survivor" vs "striver" environmental cues.
- **Rule:** A high-noise environment invalidates the probe. Control the room to control the measurement.

## 4) Step-by-step (universal execution)

1. **Define the mission** (one sentence).
2. **Choose the system + variable to test** (latent variable + proxy).
3. **Write the hypothesis** (X/Y/Z/O/R).
4. **Design the probe** (controls, risks, ethics line, stopping rule).
5. **Deploy** once in a controlled window.
6. **Measure O** on a schedule (T+30m / T+2h / T+24h).
7. **Update**: repeat/scale or change ONE variable.
8. **Convert (sales)** using micro-commitments (ask 1 input → deliver 1 small output → offer).

## 5) Non-negotiable rule

**No stories without measurement.** If you can’t observe it or proxy it, it stays a hypothesis.
